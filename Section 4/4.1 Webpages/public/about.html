About Me

In my restless dreams, I see that town, Silent Hill. You promised me you'd take me there again someday. But you never did. Well, I'm alone there now... In our "special place." I'm waiting for you. Come to see me, James. I've been waiting for you to come to see me. I'm afraid I may be losing my mind, James. I don't remember much. Mostly, I remember the doctors and nurses. They scare me. But I'm waiting for you, James. I want you to come and see me. Please come and see me.

The weight of the letter feels like paper and ash, a ghost of communication. The words themselves are fragile, reflecting Mary's failing health and the desperate, loving grasp she maintains on their shared past. You see, I'm dying, James. I am so happy I got to spend those years with you. We had such a wonderful time. But the sickness is eating me up inside. I want you to live for yourself, James. When I'm gone... I want you to stop hurting and start living again. Be gentle, James, and remember all the good times we shared.

The final paragraph hangs heavy, a stark realization that the narrative established by the first lines is impossible. The paper in his hand is the last physical connection to her, and yet, the content remains a cruel paradox reflecting the main character's denial. The ink bleeds slightly on the cheap stationary. Though the physical body is gone, the memory of her remains the strongest pull to the town. You gave me happiness. I'll take all my love to the afterlife. Thank you, James. Goodbye.
